Smart Home

A house that understands itself

For years I've been building a system that takes care of heating, lights, coffee and the daily rhythm on its own. No universal app — a home that knows my habits and notices when something is off.

What it does in practice

The home doesn't wait for me to reach for the phone. It watches multiple signals at once — whether I'm home, asleep, whether it's morning, where I'm moving — and decides on its own. A few examples:

  • 🌅 Morning at 3:15 — when the alarm rings and I move out of bed, the kitchen radio starts, the bedroom lights gently come on, the coffee is prepared. Where I'm not, lights don't turn on.
  • 🛏 Night — if I go to the bathroom, the "full morning routine" doesn't kick in. The system knows I'm just passing through and dims back down after a few minutes.
  • 🔥 Heating — each zone has its own schedule. When I'm away, temperature drops. When I come home, the bathroom warms up. More efficient than a constant 24°C all day.
  • 🚪 Coming home — phone enters range, house switches to "home" mode, audio matches time of day, blinds and windows get checked.
  • 🤖 Anomalies — when something's off (alarm at 3am with no motion anywhere, or lights staying on while I'm away), the system notices and sends me a push.

The goal: less switch-clicking, more calm. The house understands itself.

What it controls

🔥

Heating & climate

4 zones with their own schedules. Three modes (Auto / Manual / Off). More efficient than one button for the whole house.

💡

Lights

Dim based on time of day and outdoor light. When I'm away or asleep, they turn themselves off.

🔊

Audio & morning routine

Kitchen radio, gentle briefing, music per scene. Never plays when I'm sleeping.

🛡

Security

Motion, doors, windows, cameras. When something's off-normal, I get a phone alert.

⚡

Energy

Tracks appliance consumption. I see in advance how many kWh the boiler, fridge or stove uses.

📊

Dashboards

Three displays (big monitor, laptop, wall-mounted RPi panel in the hallway). Whole-house state at a glance.

Under the hood (for techies)

Built on Homey Pro 2026 + a custom HomeyScript layer. Architecture: request-first pipeline (scripts ask, router decides), device map as the single source of truth for device names, multi-signal sleep/wake decision-making, a guard layer for anomaly and regression detection. Logs and technical data are stored locally on a Raspberry Pi / SSD (JSONL + SQLite). Mobile notifications via Homey notifications API.
