Finance & investing

A personal view on finance

I enjoy finance because I want to understand where my money goes. Investing isn't a sprint — I'm more interested in understanding principles than chasing short-term speculation. This page is an educational launcher, not financial advice.

⚠️ Disclaimer: This page is a personal view and a technical demo. It is not investment advice. All numbers in examples are illustrative and serve only to explain the principle.

Who this page is for: anyone who wants to orient long-term in their own finances — ETFs, stocks, crypto, gold — without pressure of "buy this right now". 11 chapters and an interactive bookkeeping demo.

What you won't find here: specific investment tips, my portfolio numbers, affiliate links. No "this will be the next Bitcoin". It's an educational launcher, not a signal.

💭 How I think about money

Personal view — what finances mean to me and what I decide by.

1. How I think about finances

I treat finance as a long-term system, not a series of quick decisions. If I wrote it top-down, it goes something like this:

  1. Reserve — before anything else. Peace before forced sales.
  2. Regularity — monthly rhythm beats one-off actions.
  3. Understanding risk — what I can lose and whether I can take it.
  4. Tool selection — broad ETFs, stocks, gold, cash, crypto.
  5. Speculation — only at the end and only from a portion I can afford to lose.

To all this I add four principles: patience, regularity, record-keeping and discipline. Understanding risk matters more than finding the one right stock.

💡 Terms briefly. DCA = regular buying regardless of price. HODL = patient holding (from a typo of "hold"). ETF = a basket of many stocks at once (e.g. "the whole US market"). Stock picking = manually chosen individual companies.

4. Useful calculators

  • Compound interest — what regular monthly deposits do at X% over Y years.
  • Reserve — how much I need to cover 3–6 months of expenses.
  • Currency conversion — when buying foreign ETFs / stocks.
  • Goal time — when do I reach my goal at the current monthly deposit.

Several free online calculators do this. I won't link them here to avoid linking to a service that may go offline next year.

5. Checklist before buying a stock

  1. Do I understand what the company does and how it makes money?
  2. Would I want to own it for 10+ years if I couldn't sell?
  3. Do I know its main risks (regulation, competition, debt)?
  4. Did I read the last annual report (at least the summary)?
  5. Did I check the P/E and how it compares to industry peers?
  6. Am I willing to lose this money if I'm wrong?
  7. Is it a position I can monitor without obsessing daily?
💡 If I can't answer "yes" to at least 5 out of 7, I don't buy. I write it in the journal anyway.

6. Checklist before buying an ETF

  1. What does the ETF track? (S&P 500 / MSCI World / sector / ...)
  2. What's the TER (total expense ratio)? Anything over 0.5% deserves a second look.
  3. Is it accumulating (reinvests dividends) or distributing? — tax implications.
  4. Domicile (Ireland / Luxembourg) — affects taxation.
  5. Size of fund — under €100M is more at risk of closure.
  6. Replication: physical (holds actual stocks) or synthetic (swap with bank — extra counterparty risk).

7. Crypto without rose-tinted glasses

Crypto interests me as a phenomenon and as a small portion of the portfolio. But not as a "this will get me rich quick" tool. Three things I always insist on:

  1. Only as much as I can afford to lose — typically <10% of portfolio.
  2. Cold wallet for larger amounts — Trezor or Ledger, never on the exchange long-term.
  3. Backup seed phrases offline — paper or metal plate, in two physically separate places.
⚠️ Watch out for: "guaranteed yield" services, copy-trading bots, influencers with TG groups, "the next 1000×" coins. 99% of those end up being someone else's profit on your loss.

8. Taxes and record-keeping

  • 3-year time test on shares and crypto — if held over 3 years, gain is tax-free in CZ (still valid as of 2026).
  • Dividend tax — depends on country of origin (CZ 15%, US can have 30% without W-8BEN).
  • Keep records of every buy/sell with date, amount, price — even if not selling now.
  • Crypto-to-crypto swap is also a taxable event.

For larger amounts consult a tax advisor. I'm not one.

9. Investor security minimum

  1. 2FA on every broker / exchange — preferably hardware key or authenticator app, not SMS.
  2. Unique password per service — password manager makes this easy.
  3. Backup seeds and recovery codes offline.
  4. Watch for phishing — fake broker emails, fake "tax authority" messages, fake support tickets.
  5. Don't follow random "tips" on social media — most are paid promotion or pump & dump.

10. Monthly investment routine

  1. Check reserve — still 3–6 months of expenses?
  2. Regular ETF buy on the agreed day (DCA).
  3. Update journal — what bought, what watching, what reading.
  4. Check pending dividends and reinvestment.
  5. 15 min news read — but no impulse decisions.

11. Annual audit

  1. Total balance change vs last year (real return after inflation).
  2. Allocation review — has any class drifted too much?
  3. Costs check — fees, FX, custodian, taxes.
  4. Tax doc preparation (broker reports + crypto exports).
  5. Goal recheck — is the plan still realistic?
