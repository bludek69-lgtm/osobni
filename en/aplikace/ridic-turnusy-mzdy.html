🚌Driver work — shifts & salary

Local tool for a bus driver (internally "BudLine Panel"). Parses shift schedules from PDF, computes monthly salary from driven shifts, builds an own payslip and compares it to the official one. Includes a tax layer (advance tax, insurance, net pay) and yearly rate verification. Fully offline, no cloud.

Python Local web (stdlib http.server) PDF shift parser Salary engine No cloud

← Back to My apps

What it does

Why it exists: every month I get a shift schedule and a payslip. Manually checking whether hours, bonuses (night, weekend, holidays), waiting time and net pay match is tedious. This app does it for me — locally, on my PC, no cloud.

How it works: it reads the shift PDFs (splits them into runs, times, km), computes the gross part from confirmed driven shifts via an own salary engine and builds an own (computed) payslip. It then puts it next to the official payslip from the carrier (from PDF) and compares them line by line — with a clear verdict MATCHES / WATCH / MISMATCH and an explanation per row. If only part of the month is entered, the app detects it and reports WATCH (incomplete data), not a false "mismatch".

Taxes & net pay: a separate tax layer computes advance income tax, social and health insurance and net pay — strictly from manually confirmed rates (the app never invents rates; until confirmed, net pay waits). Credits and children come from the "driver card".

Key features

  • Shift parser (PDF) — Reads the monthly schedule, parses runs, times, stops and km (incl. long-distance lines and deadhead transfers).
  • Salary engine — From confirmed shifts computes hours, waiting time and bonuses (night, weekend, holiday, hard-environment).
  • Own vs official payslip + comparison — Builds the own payslip and compares it to the official one; 9 states (matches / differs / missing / unclear / …), verdict MATCHES/WATCH/MISMATCH, with source and what to add per item.
  • Tax layer (net pay) — Advance tax, social and health insurance, net pay — only from manually confirmed rates; rates locked against overwrite.
  • Driver card — Children, taxpayer credit, signed declaration, insurer — inputs to the calculation.
  • Yearly rate verification — One button checks official sources and offers rates to confirm (they never change by themselves).
  • Records — Shifts + AETR/561 checks, fleet, drivers, collective agreement, updates from official sources.
  • Local, no cloud — No account, no outbound API. Payslips and schedules stay on my PC.

Status

Working (v1.0) — private tool, no public hosting. The main flow shift → official payslip → own payslip → comparison → net pay works. Built strictly safe: the salary engine is never edited, everything is a proposal, real data is never overwritten automatically, with a backup and a check at each phase. Tax rates are confirmed manually from official sources — the app never invents them or presents them as certainty (a checking aid, not tax advice).

📖 Dev diary — Driver work — shifts & salary

  • Goal — A local driver tool: shifts → pay → payslip check. Fully offline, as a checking aid.
  • Shift parser — Reading shifts from PDF; fixed long-distance runs (4-digit numbers) and deadhead transfers so km and runs match the original.
  • Dual payslip + comparison — Official payslip (from PDF) vs own (computed) via the existing salary engine; line-by-line comparison with 9 states and a MATCHES/WATCH/MISMATCH verdict. Incomplete month = WATCH, not a false "mismatch".
  • Tax layer — A separate engine for advance tax, social/health insurance and net pay; rates only from manually confirmed rules, locked against overwrite. Validated against a real payslip (insurance and tax matched; the gap was untaxed per-diems).
  • Driver card — Children, credit, declaration → inputs to the calculation. One driver per instance (a colleague gets their own copy).
  • Yearly rate verification — A button that checks official sources and offers rates to confirm; they never change by themselves.
  • Status — Working v1.0; fine-tuning (per-diems in the net comparison) once the first full real month is entered.
