Collection — Numismatics & Philately

🪙Collection — Numismatics & Philately

A personal catalogue of coins, banknotes, stamps and postcards with an auction-house look. AI recognizes an item from a photo and fills in the details, the app shows where the same item actually sells (marketplaces and auctions) and tracks what raises or lowers the value. Real data stays local with me — no cloud, no accounts, no payments.

HTML/JS Python + FastAPI SQLite AI Gemini (vision) Windows (.exe, Tauri)

← Back to My apps

What it does

Why it exists: a friend from work collects coins and stamps as a hobby and kept asking me questions — what is this piece, where do I look it up, what does it sell for. So I built him an app to make it clear: photograph the item, the app helps identify it and give an indicative valuation, and links straight to places where the price can be verified. Runs locally, looks like an auction-house catalogue, no account, no data leaves the machine.

Two modes: a public demo (sample data only, runs in the browser) and the full version on my PC with its own database, photos and AI. The real collection never leaves the computer.

AI recognition from a photo: photograph a coin or stamp, the app sends the photo to AI (Gemini) which reads the text on the item and pre-fills the form — category, country, year, denomination, condition. Then you just review and save. It handles both sides at once (obverse + reverse). If the photo is blurry or it is not a coin, the app fills nothing and asks for a new photo (like document verification).

Where it sells: I browse catalogues and shops (Numista, Pofis, Colnect, Delcampe…) right inside the app — each result shows the name, price and image, and prices in foreign currencies are converted to Czech koruna using the Czech National Bank rate. Each item also keeps a price history over time, so I can see where the value is heading. You see immediately what the same item actually sells for — not what someone is asking.

Help for beginners: a dedicated page explains the AI (Gemini) in plain language — how to get a free key and what it costs — plus shooting with a phone, backups and security. The AI key can be entered right in the app and stays only on the computer.

What drives the price: an educational section — what raises value (higher grade, mint errors, certification, NH gum on stamps) and what lowers it (cleaning, mounting holes, defects). So you never buy or sell blind.

Bulk import: if you already keep a list in Excel, upload it in one go (CSV / XLSX) instead of clicking item by item.

Key features

  • Auction-house style catalogue — dark look with gold accents, item detail as an auction "lot" (large photo, parameters, valuation).
  • AI recognition from photo — Gemini reads the text on the coin/stamp and pre-fills the fields; obverse + reverse in one step; photo quality check (bad photo = no valuation).
  • Catalogues right inside the app — browse Czech and foreign catalogues and shops (coins, stamps) in the app window: name, price, image, link to source. Foreign prices converted to koruna (CNB rate), per-item price history over time. Rule: "a realized price beats an asking price".
  • Help for beginners — a page explaining the AI key (Gemini), shooting photos, backups and security; every place that connects to the internet/AI has an ℹ button with an explanation.
  • What drives the price — up/down factors separately for coins and stamps + how a price is composed (catalogue vs. auction vs. estimate).
  • Valuation with evidence — attach a source to each item (catalogue / realized auction / appraisal); active listings are excluded from the average.
  • Item photos — front/back, shown in the card and the detail.
  • Import / export — bulk import from CSV/Excel, export to CSV / Excel / PDF / ZIP.
  • Local and safe — SQLite + photos only on my PC; only code and sample data go to GitHub. The Windows app (Tauri) starts its backend itself with one click.
